Blogging and Public Engagement

Each platform a writer uses appeals to a slightly different audience. I write blog posts on academic and non-academic sites largely for the purpose of engaging with a wider, non-academic audience (that, and it's faster than traditional print publishing). I started writing for the North American Society for Studies in Romanticism Graduate Caucus Blog in 2012. Later, I was selected as a Communications Fellow for the Keats-Shelley Assocaition of America, where my primary responsibility was producing content for our Twitter account, but I also interviewed scholars and edited those interviews for the K-SAA Blog. I've continued to do a little work for the Keats-Shelley Association, but most recently I've starting blogging on Medium about my job search, trying to balance the hunt for academic and non-academic jobs, and basically all-things related to the existential crisis that is being in your late 30s with a dissertation, publications, ten years of teaching experience, and yet, no full-time job.
