Teaching Practice

I specialize in media/adaptation studies, which informs my pedagogical approach. Adaptations are a creative way to help students transfer unfamiliar information to familiar contexts. In my “Alien Minds” course, students might adapt poems from Wordsworth’s Lyrical Ballads to a video game platform that blends text, historical context, and analysis, paired with an essay exploring how the adaptation opens a new point of access to a topic or problem. Not only do these exercises mirror class content, they are also embedded with lessons in metacognition. Such exercises will help students think about how adaptations allow thinking to migrate across domains. By adding rhetorical tools, they also learn a vocabulary to critique and improve how science is communicated to the public.
