Teaching Philosophy

My teaching is inquiry-based and student-centered. While my classes are peppered with lectures, for the most part students can expect to arrive to class with a hands-on activity waiting for them, followed by group discussion and written reflection, often in that order. This process is informed by my research on the history of math, logic, and education. Instead of starting with an abstract discussion, students begin with concrete materials, on which our discussion is then based. Accordingly, students have a chance to work out problems for themselves (with guidance, of course), followed by analysis and philosophical reflection. My classes emphasize formulating questions, thinking about process, discovery, problem solving, creativity, and collaboration.

Creativity in the Classroom

My specialization in media/adaptation studies also informs my pedagogical approach. Adaptations are a creative way to help students transfer unfamiliar information to familiar contexts. In my literature courses, students might adapt a poem from Wordsworthâ€™s Lyrical Ballads to a video game platform that blends text, historical context, and analysis, paired with an essay that explores how the adaptation opens a new point of access. These exercises are also embedded with lessons in metacognition. They help students think about how adaptations allow thinking to migrate across domains. Pedagogical Research and Training

To bring my love of teaching to others, I strive to balance my research agenda with publications on pedagogy. At present, I am writing about digital mapping tools for online teaching. In the past, I have written on adaptation exercises in face-to-face literature classes.

Since joining the faculty at Seattle University in 2019, I have participated in several courses at the university's Center for Digital Learning and Innovation, where I have concentrated on online course design and pedagogy. I also completed Highline College's Summer Institute (2019), where I focused on creating accessible syllabi to comply with Title IX guidelines.
