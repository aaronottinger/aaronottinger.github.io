Teaching Philosophy

My teaching is inquiry-based and student-centered. While my classes are peppered with lectures, for the most part students can expect to arrive to class with a hands-on activity waiting for them, followed by group discussion and written reflection, often in that order. This process is informed by my research on the history of math, logic, and education. Instead of starting with an abstract discussion, students begin with concrete materials, on which our discussion is then based. Accordingly, students have a chance to work out problems for themselves (with guidance, of course), followed by analysis and philosophical reflection. My classes emphasize formulating questions, thinking about process, discovery, problem solving, creativity, and collaboration.
