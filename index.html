About

My research focuses on the co-evolution of human cognitive processes and text technologies. I ground this research in the study of eighteenth- and nineteenth-century British literature and media, largely because this was the era when modern mathematics were being formalized, and it is through literature that these new approaches to knowing and being migrated to a wider reading public.

To learn more about my research and for a list of publications, please visit this page.

My teaching is inquiry-based and student-centered. I also specialize in hybrid and online formats, and I currently serve as the New and Continuing Studies liaison to the Center for Digital Learning and Innovation at Seattle U. In this capacity, I assist intructors with their online classes and make suggestions to the Center for improving Seattle U's online education more generally. Regardless of the format, my classes emphasize asking questions, recursive processes, discovery, problem solving, creativity, and collaboration.

To learn more about my teaching and to download course materials, please visit this page.
