Research Focus

Is it possible to view the world objectively? What about the human mind? And if everything is already determined, then how is change even possible? These are the kinds of questions I address in my research, which focuses on the dialogue between psychology and mathematics as it appeared in literature, from the end of the seventeenth century to the dawn of the Victorian period. While I am fascinated by the historical relationship between mathematics, media (especially books & literature), and mind, I also strive to connect this historical narrative to present-day attempts to ascertain and theorize objective standpoints in the humanities and the sciences. In my investigations, I am equally committed to promoting the artistic images that emerge as a result of pursuing the "view from nowhere," as well as critiquing colonialist ideologies that often lie at the heart of the pursuit.
