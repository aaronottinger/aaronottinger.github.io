Welcome to the professional website of Aaron Ottinger. Here I detail my past, current, and upcoming research and teaching. The site is currently undergoing reconstruction, so please pardon the mess.

To a large extent, my research focuses on the intersection of literature and mathematics. I'm particularly curious about the way the relationship between math and literature conditions how we read, and ultimately, how we think and feel.

I am also working on a project about the "global ecodigital divide." This study investigates the long historical connections between technology, systemic racism, and ecological destruction. It also offers multimodal and multimedial projects to help students better understand these connections (and to do something about them!).

To learn more about my research and for a list of publications, please visit this page.

I teach courses in litearture, rhetoric and composition, digtal/media studies, and digital humanities. I am trained to teach online and in person. In all of my classes, I emphasize inquiry, recursive processes, equity, problem solving, accessibility, creativity, and collaboration.

To learn more about my teaching and to download course materials, please visit this page.
