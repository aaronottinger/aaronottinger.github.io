About

To a large extent, my research focuses on the intersection of literature and mathematics. I'm particularly curious about the way the relationship between math and literature conditions how we read, and ultimately, how we think and feel.

More recently, my interests have turned towards digital culture. I focus on what I call the "global ecodigital divide." This study investigates the long historical connections between technology, systemic racism, and ecological destruction, and it offers multimodal and multimedial projects to help people better understand and resist this ongoing problem.

To learn more about my research and for a list of publications, please visit this page.

My teaching is inquiry-based and student-centered. In addition to face-to-face classes, I also specialize in hybrid and online formats. In all of my classes, I emphasize inquiry, recursive processes, equity, problem solving, accessibility, creativity, and collaboration.

To learn more about my teaching and to download course materials, please visit this page.
