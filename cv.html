Bio

The many interests of my past have come to inform my research and pedagogical strategies, forming a blend of critical and creative ways to think, feel, read, and write. From a childhood filled with drawing, many cartoon teaching documents have followed; I often include graphic novels in my reading lists; and in many cases, I have encouraged students to access literary texts by adapting them to comic book form. Even the theater plays an important role in my teaching, as I appeal to students' reasoning faculties as well as their emotions--not to mention integrating the occasional in-class skit. My current research remains focused on the legacy of the Enlightenment, but I am now investigating how we mathematize everyday human life. Of course, taking this direction is not to say that I disavow math and science. On the contrary, I approach these fields with wonder and respect. But, as the cautionary tale of Mary Shelley's Frankenstein tells us, just because we can do something does not mean we should.

The views expressed on this site do not represent the views of my employers.
